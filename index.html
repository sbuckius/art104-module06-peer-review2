Introduction & thesis

Part 2

Does the thesis clearly state what the project is about and why it matters? Is it specific enough to give the project direction — not just a broad topic?

Body — ideas and structure

Part 3

Does each section of the project have one clear main idea? Is it clear what each section will discuss and why it matters to the project as a whole? Is the order logical?

Within each theme, do the artworks, sources, and ideas hold together? Or does a theme mix ideas that don't quite belong with each other? Go through each theme and consider whether everything in it is pointing in the same direction.

How clearly does the project communicate its central ideas and their connection to the speculative artwork?

Artwork analysis

Part 3 cont.

Does the project indicate that artworks will be described in enough detail? Is there a plan for genuine visual analysis — not just summarizing what the work is?

Are the artworks clearly linked to the project's central ideas — or just listed? Does each artwork appear to illuminate something meaningful about the project's themes?

Do each of the artworks discussed relate directly to the speculative artwork? Can you see a clear line from each artwork to the speculative piece — in terms of theme, medium, or idea?

Are the chosen artworks appropriate and relevant? Do they genuinely support the project's focus or feel loosely connected?

Technology and digital media

Part 3 cont.

Is the technology (e.g. AI, AR, VR, generative systems, networks, etc.) identified and explained in the project? Does the plan indicate it will be explained clearly enough for a non-specialist?

Does the project weave the technology into its discussions of artworks and ideas — or is it treated as a standalone explainer section? Does understanding the technology feel essential to understanding the project?

Does the technology discussed relate directly to the speculative artwork proposed? Is it clear how the technology informs or enables the speculative piece?

Are key digital media concepts (e.g. interactivity, virtuality, data, code, networked culture) engaged with meaningfully in the project?

Research and sources

Part 3 cont.

Are at least 3 sources identified, with at least 2 being peer-reviewed? Are the non-art sources relating to a topic outside of art (science, technology, sociology, psychology, etc.)?

Does the project show where sources will be used to support or develop specific ideas? Is it clear how each source connects to the project's themes — or are they just listed?

Do the articles and outside-field sources relate directly to the speculative artwork — not just to the artworks discussed? Is it clear how the research informs the proposed speculative piece?

Are the non-art sources genuinely from a different discipline — and do they appear to meaningfully enrich the project's ideas rather than just being cited?

Are citations formatted consistently in ASA or Chicago style? Are in-text citations and the reference list correct?

Speculative artwork

Is a speculative artwork proposed in the project? Does it feel like a genuine culmination of the project's ideas — not just an afterthought?

The project should have three main themes or body sections. Does each theme relate directly to the speculative artwork — not just to the other artworks discussed? Can you trace a clear line from each theme to the speculative piece?

Does the speculative artwork feel like a logical extension of the artworks and ideas in the project? Is its connection to the thesis explicit?

Is the technology or medium for the speculative artwork clearly described in the project? Is it clear what kind of artwork this would be?

Does the project include at least 3 sketches per group member for the speculative artwork?

Are sketches or visual references for the speculative artwork included?
